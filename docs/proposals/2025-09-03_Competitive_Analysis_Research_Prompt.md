# Research Prompt for Google Gemini 2.5 Pro
**Subject:** Competitive Analysis of Modern Bible Study & Etymology Software for LogoMesh Feature Gaps

**Objective:** To identify unique feature opportunities and user experience gaps in the current market of Bible study software that LogoMesh can fulfill. This report will guide the prioritization and design of the Book Ingestion and Analysis feature.

**Software for Analysis:**
Please analyze the features and user experience of the following key software. Include both established desktop applications and modern web/mobile platforms.
- Logos Bible Software
- Accordance Bible Software
- e-Sword
- BibleWorks (Legacy, but influential)
- Blue Letter Bible (Web/Mobile App)
- YouVersion (Bible.com)
- Any other significant open-source or academic tools you can identify.

**Key Areas for Analysis:**

1.  **Interlinear & Lexical Tools:**
    -   How is interlinear (original language + English gloss) data presented? Is it static text or interactive?
    -   How deep is the etymological and morphological information provided for each word?
    -   What are the common UI/UX patterns for exploring this data, and what are their limitations?

2.  **Semantic & Thematic Search:**
    -   What are the capabilities of their search engines? Is it limited to keywords and phrases, or do they offer more advanced semantic or conceptual search (e.g., "find verses about 'covenant' that don't use the word 'covenant'")?
    -   How are search results presented and filtered?

3.  **Cross-Reference & Intertextuality:**
    -   How are connections between different biblical passages surfaced to the user?
    -   Is this based on manually curated cross-reference lists, or are there any tools that use computational methods to suggest novel connections?

4.  **Knowledge Graph & Entity Exploration:**
    -   Do these tools model the relationships between entities (people, places, events)?
    -   How can users explore this network? Is it through hyperlinks, dedicated visualizations, or other means?

5.  **User Experience & Feature Gaps:**
    -   Based on your analysis (and any available user reviews or forum discussions), what are the most common complaints or points of friction for users of these tools?
    -   What features are consistently requested but not yet implemented?
    -   Where are the significant opportunities for innovation? What could a tool built from the ground up in 2025 with modern AI and database technology do that these older tools cannot?

**Final Output:**
Please provide a report that synthesizes your findings and concludes with a clear, actionable list of "Feature Gaps & Opportunities" for LogoMesh. This list should guide the development of a unique and compelling product.
